How to get a dump of serial debug output?

up vote 2 down vote favorite

I have a13-olinuxino and try to boot it from usb-stick. In my possession there is a computer and cables.

I need to get a dump of serial debug output during boot.

How do I get it?

bootloader olinuxino serial-debug

There is an UART1 connector on the board, according to the datasheet/manual it allows outputting of a certain amount of debugging information. It is very probable that this is turned on by default, since the most Google posts I find are for turning it off to use it for something else.

The signals are TTL 3.3V level, so you need a chip that either turns it up to +/-10V for RS232 connectors at the back of your PC, such as the well known MAX232 chip (though you might need a special edition for 3.3V).

You can also hook up a TTL-level USB-to-Serial cable such as those little diddies made by FTDI or clones thereof and just connect that directly, but only if it is 3.3V. Using a 5V TTL level cable will destroy your ARM.

I looked at the pages 21 and 22 of the linked PDF and the first 10 synopses on Google Search for this answer, I am not an active user of this board. But it might tide you over until a pro comes along. Or it might actually probably be sufficient.

It depends on how the linux image on the USB flash device is configured. Some will output debug information to one of the two serial ports on the board. Others will output debug information to a USB connected debugger.

Without knowing what linux image you are using, though, it's impossible to tell.

Olimex sells the USB-SERIAL-CABLE-F which connects to the UART1 and allows console access for most of their images. If you are using a Linux image from them, chances are good that this cable will allow you to see all console output.

@skrrgwasme That is incorrect. The BeagleBone Black uses a version of UBoot that is specifically configured to provide debug output via a specific UART. The Olimex board mentioned here can be configured with UBoot, and can output similar data once UBoot starts, but the CPUs themselves do not provide that on boot-up. You have to boot into something first to configure the UART and output the debugging info.
