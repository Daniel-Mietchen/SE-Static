How to optimize Beaglebone Black power up sequence for low power?

up vote 1 down vote favorite

About a year ago I was working on a project using beagle bone black. I was able to bootup the beaglebone black single board computer with a USB cable without any issues. But when I attached a 4 inch LCD display module from 4D Systems the beagle bone black required the use of an external power supply. I used a power supply with around 850ma. In summary

  • Beaglebone black + 4in LCD from 4D Systems require 850ma or less. I am startup maximum of around 550mA, steady stateless than 500mA

  • Beaglebone black required 500ma or less. I measured a start current around 350ma

    Beaglebone Black Single board computer

Question: I would like to get the beagle bone black + 4” LCD from 4D systems to work with 500mA or less (Power from USB). I would like to investigate the software for the power up sequence with the intent to optimize power up. So has anyone investigated the power up sequence for a beagle bone black? If so can you please share some insight? Boot up time is less important.

References:

  • BeagleBone Black
  • 4D 4.3” LCD CAPE Beagle Bone Black 4.3” LCD CAPE
